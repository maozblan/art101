a little about imaginary numbers B)

Imaginary numbers are this silly little thing that actually isn't imaginary. Instead, they are an ordered pair of two real numbers split into two parts: real and imaginary. In math, they do a lot to help us solve problems that have to be otherwise estimated, and the coolest part is that those problems don't even have to have imaginary numbers as their answers! One way I like to think about it is that "imaginary numbers" are imaginary because they are of a higher dimension where two of our "3D numbers" is one of theirs. And because we are of a lower dimension, we can only see bits and pieces, thus imagining what these higher dimension numbers are... that makes it sound complicated, doesn't it... oops.
