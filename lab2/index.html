a little about quantum mechanics B)

The peculiar idea of "quantum engtanglement" is enough to boggle some minds. Perhaps the idea that two quantum particles, no matter the distance, is forever entangled in a state where if one particle changes in some form, the other particle will reflect that. And the worst part? Any measurement of entangled particles would collapse the whole system. All we might ever know, for now, is that if one particle's spin direction is left, the other must be right. So given a random quantum particle, the chance of it spinning left is 50%.
